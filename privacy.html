Privacy Policy for Tilly

Last updated: 8 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Tilly app (“Tilly”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) handles information when you use it. Tilly is designed to be private by default. Please read this policy alongside our gentle in-app reminder that Tilly provides general information only and is not a medical service.

1. Who we are

Tilly is provided by Tilly Health, based in Queensland, Australia. If you have any questions about this policy or your privacy, contact us at tillyhealth@protonmail.com.

2. The short version

  • Tilly stores the information you enter — such as your due date, journal entries, saved baby names, and appointments — locally on your own device.
  • We do not collect that information, transmit it to our servers, or have any ability to see it. We do not operate a server that receives your personal data.
  • We do not sell your information to anyone, and Tilly does not show ads.
  • If you delete the app, or use the “erase all data” option in Settings, that information is removed from your device.

3. Information Tilly handles, and where it stays

When you use Tilly you may enter information such as:

  • your estimated due date or last period date;
  • whether you are expecting multiples;
  • journal entries (mood, symptoms, weight, notes);
  • baby names you save to your shortlist;
  • appointments you add, and any reminders you set;
  • your postcode, when you search for nearby services;
  • your choice to pause the app or to indicate a pregnancy loss.

All of this is stored only in local storage on your device. It is not sent to us or to any third party by the app. We have no access to it and cannot retrieve, view, or recover it.

Because this information stays on your device, its security also depends on your device’s own protections (such as your screen lock and device encryption).

4. Information we do NOT collect

Tilly, as currently released, does not:

  • create an account or require you to log in;
  • collect your name, email, or contact details through the app;
  • track your location in the background;
  • use third-party advertising or sell data to advertisers;
  • include third-party analytics or tracking software that identifies you.

5. Features that open other apps on your device

Some features hand off to other apps already on your phone. When you do this, you are leaving Tilly and interacting with that other app or service under its own terms and privacy practices. Specifically:

  • Calling a service or helpline opens your phone’s dialer with the number.
  • “Map” for a service opens your maps app with the location’s name.
  • Appointment reminders use your device’s own local notification system; reminders are scheduled on your device and are not sent through us.
  • “Go deeper” and course links open your web browser at a third-party website (such as Raising Children Network, Services Australia, or a first aid provider). Those websites have their own privacy policies.

Tilly does not control, and is not responsible for, the privacy practices of your phone’s dialer, maps, browser, or other apps and websites you choose to open.

6. Bundled information (not about you)

Tilly includes reference information built into the app — such as food guidance, baby facts, baby names, newborn guides, and a directory of public services and helplines. This content is general information drawn from public and official Australian sources. It is not personal information and does not identify you.

7. Children

Tilly is intended for expectant and new parents and is not directed at children.

8. Your control over your information

  • You can edit or delete individual entries (journal items, appointments, saved names) within the app.
  • You can use Settings → “erase all my data” to remove the information Tilly has stored on your device.
  • You can uninstall Tilly, which removes its locally stored data from your device.

9. Data security

We have designed Tilly to minimise privacy risk by keeping your information on your device rather than transmitting or storing it centrally. No method of electronic storage is perfectly secure, and we encourage you to protect your device with a screen lock and keep your operating system up to date.

10. Changes to this policy

If we change how Tilly handles information — for example, if we introduce optional features that involve sending data off your device — we will update this policy and change the “Last updated” date above. Where a change is significant, we will make reasonable efforts to bring it to your attention within the app.

11. Your rights and how to contact us

Australian privacy law (including the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles) gives individuals rights over their personal information. Because Tilly does not collect your personal information onto our systems, we generally hold nothing about you to access or correct. If you have any question, concern, or complaint about privacy and Tilly, please contact us at tillyhealth@protonmail.com.

We will respond within a reasonable time. If you are not satisfied, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.

12. A note on health information

Some information you choose to record in Tilly (such as symptoms) may be health information. Tilly keeps this on your device and does not collect it. Tilly provides general information only and is not a medical service; it does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your midwife, doctor, or child health nurse about your own health and your baby’s health, and call 000 in an emergency.
